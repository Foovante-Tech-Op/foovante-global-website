Our mission
Africa holds 10% of global carbon credit potential and now issues close to 5% as of 2025. We exist to help close that gap further.

The voluntary carbon market is on track to reach $50–$200B by 2030, and African ecosystems (forests, mangroves, drylands, agroforestry systems) are the highest-quality natural carbon assets on Earth. They should be the supply side of that market.

They are not. Today, African projects generate fewer than 2% of global credits, because the verification, certification, and trading infrastructure was built for the global North, not for a cooperative in Rwanda or a community forest in Ghana.

Foovante is building that infrastructure from Accra. Continuous satellite monitoring, on-the-ground verification by local agents, multi-standard issuance, and a marketplace that pays projects most of what their credits sell for. We are building the operating system for African carbon. And we are building it from the continent itself.

What we do

Three products. One stack.

Foovante operates as a vertically integrated platform: origination, verification, and distribution all under one roof. It is the only way to keep quality high and economics fair.
